Sometime last year I became an adjunct professor at University of Copenhagen's Department of Sociology. And just to be clear: I'm still primarily the Technical University of Denmark. The adjunct position is more of a way of signaling that I work closely with social science researchers (e.g. through my associate director position at [SODAS](http://sodas.ku.dk)).
Anyway, the important thing here is that **I'm finally giving my inaugural lecture**. The lecture is a fun chance for me to reflect on what's happened up to now. My goal is to make the lecture be fun, entertaining, and personal (in a way that I hope will shed light on the mechanics of the scientific process). I hope you'll come and see it. 
Here are the details:

- **Date & Time**: Friday April 20th
- **Location**: *Room 35.01.44*, University of Copenhagen. [*It's not super easy to find building 35, so here's special directions: The easiest way is to go to [Gammeltoftgade 15, Copenhagen K](https://goo.gl/maps/kmnEzth5mam) and enter the brand-new building (Building 35), then head to the basement & follow the signs to 35.01.44*]
