I recently went to the excellent [GESIS CSS Winter Symposium](http://www.gesis.org/css-wintersymposium/program/) in Cologne. The symposium was brilliantly organized by [Markus Strohmaier](http://markusstrohmaier.info), who has grown it into a major Computational Social Science event (this year 290 participants) within a very short time. So many interesting people to talk to!
My talk was about *Fundamental Structures of Complex Social Network*and based on forthcoming work with [Vedran Sekara](https://twitter.com/vedransekara). I had a nice timeslot and received lots of Twitter love (reproduced below for easy access), which - I  have to admit - feels pretty darn great when you've worked hard to create some exciting science!
